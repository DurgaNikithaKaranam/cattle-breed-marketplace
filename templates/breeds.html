Indian Cattle Breeds

Gir Cow

Gir is a famous Indian dairy breed known for its high milk production and heat tolerance. It has a distinctive convex forehead and long ears. Gir cattle are disease resistant, calm in nature, and suitable for tropical climates, making them ideal for sustainable dairy farming.

Sahiwal Cow

Sahiwal is one of the best indigenous dairy breeds of India, originating from Punjab. It is highly resistant to diseases and heat stress. The breed is known for good fertility, longevity, and consistent milk production, even under poor feeding and management conditions.

Tharparkar Cow

Tharparkar is a dual-purpose breed native to the arid regions of Rajasthan. It is well adapted to harsh climates and low rainfall areas. The breed is valued for both milk production and draught power, making it suitable for rural farming systems.

Red Sindhi Cow

Red Sindhi cattle are known for their deep red coat and adaptability to hot climates. This breed produces good quality milk and has strong resistance to tropical diseases. Red Sindhi cows are commonly used in crossbreeding programs due to their hardiness and productivity.

Kankrej Cow

Kankrej is a large and powerful cattle breed native to Gujarat and Rajasthan. It is recognized for its strong build, long horns, and draught ability. The breed also produces good quantities of milk and performs well in hot and dry environments.

Ongole Cow

Ongole cattle originate from Andhra Pradesh and are famous for their strength and endurance. They are widely used for draught work and milk production. Ongole cows have strong immunity and adaptability, making them suitable for both traditional and commercial farming practices.
